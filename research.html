Previous Research

Reward-emotion interactions in facial expression categorization

Centre for Neuroscience, IISc Bangalore · 2021–2024 · PI: Dr. Srikanth Padmala

I examined how performance-contingent versus non-contingent rewards differentially modulate the categorization of emotional facial expressions. Using behavioral experiments and fMRI, we found that the reward–emotion interaction is critically dependent on contingency structure — only rewards tied to performance outcomes reliably enhance the processing of emotionally salient faces.

📄 Published in Motivation Science (2024) — view paper

Spatial navigation and memory

Centre for Neuroscience, IISc Bangalore · 2020–2021 · PI: Dr. Sachin Deshmukh

Contributed to research in the Space Lab investigating the neural bases of spatial navigation and memory encoding in rodents, using electrophysiological recordings and behavioral paradigms.

Intertemporal choice under uncertainty

CBCS, University of Allahabad · 2018–2020 · PI: Prof. Narayanan Srinivasan

My M.Sc dissertation examined how uncertainty modulates intertemporal preferences — how people choose between smaller immediate rewards and larger delayed rewards when outcomes are probabilistic. Presented at the 7th Annual Conference of Cognitive Science, IISc Bangalore (2021).
